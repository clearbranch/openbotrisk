## What it claims

- Entrepreneurial pitches vary in both verbal tactics and nonverbal behaviour.
- Figurative language such as metaphor, analogy, and anecdote can frame a venture in more familiar terms, but in this study language variation had limited effects on investment propensity.
- Gesturing had stronger positive effects than figurative language on investment judgments.
- Gestures can help potential investors form mental imagery of a product or venture.
- The study distinguishes beat gestures from ideational gestures:
  - beat gestures emphasize rhythm or points in speech
  - ideational gestures depict, symbolize, or demonstrate meaning
- Skilled use of gestures may make a venture feel more concrete and easier to imagine.
- The central mechanism proposed is mental imagery: gestures help viewers picture aspects of a venture and therefore evaluate it more favourably.

## What evidence it provides

The paper uses a mixed-methods design.

Study 1 is a qualitative field study of 17 technology entrepreneurs pitching to specialist technology investors at a regional investment forum in the North of England. The authors coded verbal and nonverbal features of the pitches, including figurative language and hand gestures.

From Study 1, the authors identify four pitching styles:
- **Literal**: low figurative language, low gesturing
- **Rhetorical**: high figurative language, low gesturing
- **Demonstrative**: low figurative language, high gesturing
- **Integrative**: high figurative language, high gesturing

Study 2 is an experiment using a 2 × 2 design:
- figurative language: high vs low
- gesture: high vs low

The experiment uses samples of professional investors and business students. Participants evaluate an entrepreneur pitching a new venture.

The paper reports that:
- figurative language had limited effects
- gesturing had stronger positive effects on propensity to invest
- mental imagery mediated the effect of gestures on investment propensity

The evidence is about human persuasion and investment judgment, not bot detection, automation, cybersecurity, browser fingerprinting, or web abuse.
